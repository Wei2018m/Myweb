About

About Me

I am a research scientist in the Harold Vance Department of Petroleum Engineering at Texas A&M University. My work lies at the intersection of computational geomechanics, reservoir simulation, and data-driven approaches to subsurface engineering.

My current research centers on leveraging distributed fiber-optic sensing technologies — particularly Rayleigh Frequency Shift Distributed Strain Sensing (RFS-DSS) — to characterize fracture geometries and optimize completion designs in unconventional reservoirs. I develop coupled geomechanics and fluid-flow models to interpret field-measured strain data and apply machine learning workflows for automatic history matching.

I have extensive experience with commercial simulation software including Schlumberger's Petrel, Intersect, Planar3D, UFM, and Visage. I also serve as a peer reviewer for academic journals including SPE Journal and Information Fusion.

Research

Research Interests

Bridging numerical simulation, fiber-optic diagnostics, and machine learning for subsurface engineering applications.

Core

Distributed Fiber-Optic Sensing

Coupled geomechanics and fluid-flow models to interpret RFS-DSS strain measurements for fracture characterization during production and shut-in.

Core

Hydraulic Fracture Characterization

Quantifying fracture geometry, height, and conductivity using strain-based diagnostics and numerical modeling in unconventional reservoirs.

Modeling

Computational Geomechanics

3D numerical modeling of stress fields, fracture interactions, and coupled fluid-geomechanical processes using DDM and commercial simulators.

Modeling

Reservoir Simulation

Integrated workflows using Petrel, Intersect, and Visage for reservoir characterization, simulation, and production forecasting.

AI/ML

Machine Learning Applications

AI-assisted workflows for automatic history matching, uncertainty quantification, and fracture property calibration using MCMC and neural network proxies.

Tools

Visualization & Software

Interactive dashboards and analysis tools using Python, Plotly, and Dash for fracture geometry visualization and engineering workflows.
