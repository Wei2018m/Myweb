01 — About

About Me

I am a research scientist working at the intersection of petroleum engineering, computational geomechanics, and data science. My work focuses on developing and validating advanced numerical methods for hydraulic fracture modeling and reservoir simulation.

I have extensive experience with commercial simulation software including Schlumberger's Petrel, Intersect, Planar3D, UFM, and Visage. I am also passionate about leveraging machine learning and data-driven approaches to solve complex subsurface engineering problems.

Beyond research, I serve as a peer reviewer for academic journals including SPE Journal and Information Fusion, contributing to maintaining scientific rigor in our field.

03 — Research

Research Interests

My research spans numerical simulation, machine learning, and petroleum engineering, with a focus on bridging computational methods and practical subsurface applications.

Hydraulic Fracturing Simulation

Developing and validating numerical models for hydraulic fracture propagation using the Displacement Discontinuity Method (DDM) and commercial simulators.

Computational Geomechanics

3D numerical modeling of stress fields, fracture interactions, and coupled fluid-geomechanical processes in subsurface formations.

Reservoir Simulation

Integrated workflows using Petrel, Intersect, and Visage for reservoir characterization, simulation, and production forecasting.

Machine Learning in Petroleum Engineering

Applying data-driven approaches including neural networks, MCMC parameter fitting, and statistical analysis to subsurface engineering problems.

Data Visualization & Software

Building interactive dashboards and analysis tools using Python, Plotly, and Dash for fracture geometry visualization and engineering workflows.

Fracture Analysis & Modeling

Validation of 3D fracture models against analytical solutions, with batch processing automation for large-scale parametric studies.
